Executive Summary

The week underscored a split reality: major advances in agentic capabilities and autonomous reasoning alongside conflicting adoption signals and mounting governance friction. Google pushed hands-off task execution into consumer Search; capital clustered into a few outsized rounds; and the EU’s GPAI provisions moved forward as the U.S. leaned into a state-led patchwork. Less favorable currents mattered too: AI-attributed layoffs, reliability concerns (quality drift, tool fragmentation), and a rise in fraud and impersonation cases.

What This Means for SMBs

Survey headlines conflict—self-reported “AI use” is up, yet many SMBs still say it’s “not relevant,” and verifiable production deployments remain limited. That data confusion can hide stalled value: pilots without instrumentation, copy tools mistaken for automation, and unclear ROI baselines. Treat this week as a reset: define what “adoption” means in your org, tag AI-assisted outputs, and track three metrics on every pilot (hours saved, error rate, cycle time). Favor tools with exportable data and clear SLAs to avoid lock-in, and start with one or two concrete workflows (customer triage, sales follow-ups, monthly reporting).
