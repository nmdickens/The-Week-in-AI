Global AI Policy & Governance

Global AI governance accelerated along two distinct and increasingly oppositional paths this week: the European pursuit of **technological sovereignty** and the U.S. debate over **environmental constraint**. The European Union formalized its **"Apply AI Strategy,"** a proactive industrial policy designed to promote European-made AI tools across critical sectors, aiming to reduce dependence on foreign tech.

Meanwhile, in the U.S., a coalition of over **200 environmental groups** issued urgent demands for Congress to block unregulated AI growth and impose strict limits on data center energy and water consumption. The final point of friction was geopolitical, as the **Nexperia halt** of critical wafer shipments to its China plant demonstrated that state policy has weaponized even foundational, non-advanced components, immediately threatening global supply chains.
